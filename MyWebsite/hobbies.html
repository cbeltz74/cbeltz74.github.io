My hobby is collecting baseball cards

I enjoyed collecting baseball cards as a teenager but slowly gave it up after starting college and a career.

For the past several years, I would buy a pack of Topps baseball cards at the start of the season or when I noticed they were on sale.

It was more of a way to see what the design looked like each year as I did not pay attention to anything happening with cards at the time.

In the past few years or so, I've started to collect again. For a while, the Minnesota Twins were not playing good baseball. But they had some prospects that everyone was excited about. That got me more interested in baseball in general.

It began with buying packs more often to see what players I could get and then grew into wanting to track down autographed cards of some of the up and coming Twins players.

I started checking eBay and for area card shops and shows around the area.

Fast forward to today and I now have quite a few different collections and other collectibles that I enjoy.

Some of the highlights are:

  • Every Topps Stadium Club Twins team set including some autographs, inserts and photo variations.
  • Twins Bowman Chrome Prospects with autographs from 2016 to today. I didn't start collecting these until last August. The only autographs I am missing since 2016 are Alex Kirilloff, Royce Lewis and Luis Arraez.
  • One Topps card for each year the Twins organization has existed.
  • Twins yearbooks. I am only missing the years of 1983, 1993, 1995, 1997 and 1998. If you have any of these years and are interesting in selling them, contact me.
  • Twins World Series Press Pins from 1987 and 1991.
  • All Star Game Press Pin from 2014 at Target Field.
